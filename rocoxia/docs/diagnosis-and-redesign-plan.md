# Rocoxia Homepage Diagnosis + Redesign Plan

Website reviewed:
- https://www.rocoxia.com/

## Executive Summary

Rocoxia has stronger brand potential than Book Nook World, but the homepage is currently underperforming because it does not make the brand proposition clear fast enough. The homepage mixes brand presentation, category browsing, direct product selling, video content, support information, and discount messaging without a strong conversion hierarchy.

The result is a homepage that looks active, but does not guide cold traffic toward a clear first action.

---

## Core Diagnosis

### 1. The homepage does not explain the product clearly enough in the first screen
Current H1 behavior is effectively brand-led:
- ROCOXIA

This creates a conversion problem for cold traffic because the brand name itself does not explain:
- what the product is
- who it is for
- why it is worth buying

For a brand-first domain like Rocoxia, the homepage must compensate with a stronger first-screen message.

### 2. Repeated promo messaging weakens clarity
The repeated discount bars and promo repetition create noise instead of urgency.

Current impression:
- too many sitewide discount reminders
- too little product understanding
- too little buying confidence

### 3. Homepage modules are not sequenced around a buying journey
The current homepage includes:
- hero/slides
- value statement
- product detail block
- watch/shop look
- bestsellers
- new arrivals
- community spotlights
- support blocks
- FAQ
- blog

The issue is not lack of content. The issue is weak hierarchy.

### 4. The page feels halfway between a brand homepage and a collection homepage
Rocoxia should be a curated brand-forward conversion site, but the homepage still behaves like a semi-catalog page.

That creates friction because:
- there is too much to process
- the user is not sure what to do first
- the site does not present a strong “why Rocoxia” story early enough

### 5. Typography and line breaks damage perceived polish
Several headings break awkwardly across lines, such as:
- Find What’s Your Favorite
- Tokyo, Japan: The Alley Waiting for the Cat
- Watch & Shop the Look

This reduces perceived quality and hurts premium brand positioning.

### 6. Too many goals compete on the homepage
The homepage currently tries to:
- promote discounts
- explain the category
- showcase a single product
- showcase videos
- push bestsellers
- push new arrivals
- show community content
- answer support questions

This is too much for first-touch cold traffic.

---

## Strategic Recommendation

## Rocoxia should become the brand-forward curated conversion site

If Book Nook World stays as the broader category / SEO site, Rocoxia should become:
- a cleaner brand-led destination
- a curated best-seller site
- a stronger paid-traffic landing environment
- a more premium gift / display / atmosphere-oriented shopping experience

That means Rocoxia homepage should emphasize:
1. What this product category is
2. Why it is emotionally appealing
3. Which few products to start with
4. Why people trust the brand
5. Why this makes a strong gift or display piece

---

## Recommended Homepage Structure

1. Announcement Bar
2. Header
3. Brand Hero
4. Quick Trust Bar
5. Best Sellers / Editor’s Picks
6. Why People Love Rocoxia
7. Gift / Display / Relaxation Use Cases
8. Social Proof / Review Cards
9. FAQ
10. Email Signup
11. Footer

---

## What should be removed or reduced

### Reduce heavily
- repeated promo banners
- repeated homepage discount text
- extra duplicate category or collection paths
- long mixed-format homepage flows
- overuse of slides

### Remove from early homepage
- large blog section
- too much support detail too early
- overloaded community section before the user understands the offer

---

## Homepage Positioning Direction

### Brand statement direction
Rocoxia should sound like:
- premium but warm
- imaginative but commercial
- gift-worthy but not childish
- creative but approachable

### Better homepage message territory
- Build a world worth displaying
- Tiny worlds, crafted by hand
- DIY builds with atmosphere, detail, and shelf presence
- A memorable gift for readers, collectors, and creative hobby lovers

---

## Conversion Direction

### Primary homepage CTA
- Shop Best Sellers

### Secondary CTA
- Explore Book Nook Kits
- Browse All Themes

### Product-card CTA
- View Product

Avoid mixing too many homepage CTA verbs such as:
- Shop now
- Add to cart
- View all
- Learn more
- Watch now

Keep the hierarchy clean.

---

## Key Messaging Pillars for Rocoxia

### 1. Display Value
These are not just kits. They become shelf-worthy decorative pieces.

### 2. Emotional Escape
These products offer a calming, hands-on creative experience.

### 3. Giftability
The category is naturally strong for gifting and should be stated directly.

### 4. Curated Selection
Rocoxia should not feel like an endless catalog. It should feel selected.

---

## What makes Rocoxia different from Book Nook World

### Book Nook World
- category-led
- SEO-led
- broader navigation
- collection-first
- educational and search-friendly

### Rocoxia
- brand-led
- paid-traffic-friendly
- curated and premium
- product-first
- stronger gift / atmosphere / design angle

---

## Recommended Homepage Copy Direction

### Hero headline direction
- Build a tiny world worth displaying.
- Tiny worlds, crafted by hand.
- DIY worlds with atmosphere, detail, and charm.

### Hero supporting copy direction
Focus on:
- gift-worthy
- calming build experience
- bookshelf / desk display
- premium detail

---

## Priority Fixes

### P1
- rewrite hero headline and subheading
- simplify top promo messaging
- move best sellers much higher
- reduce homepage clutter
- improve CTA hierarchy

### P2
- reposition community / social proof
- rewrite section headings into more native brand English
- add clearer trust language near first product block

### P3
- restructure FAQ
- refine email signup copy
- improve mobile-first headline wrapping and spacing

---

## Final Recommendation

Rocoxia should not try to be a second version of Book Nook World.

It should become:
- the cleaner brand site
- the paid traffic landing site
- the curated best-seller site
- the more premium-looking conversion environment

That is the strongest way to justify keeping both sites live.
